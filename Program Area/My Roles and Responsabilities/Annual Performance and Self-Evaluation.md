# Resources to Enhance Your Performance

Since your job is a blend of **Law, Medicine, and Administration**, "bettering your performance" usually means increasing your efficiency in managing data and people.

### 1. Specialized Training (The "Hard" Skills)

-   **NAEMSO Resources:** The _National Association of EMS Officials_ has specific resources for people in state/regional roles. Their "EMS Education" or "Regulatory" tracks are built exactly for your job description.
    
-   **Investigative Interviewing:** Since you handle complaints, a short course in "Forensic/Investigative Interviewing" (often used by HR or Law Enforcement) can help you navigate difficult conversations with providers without it becoming adversarial.
    
-   **Lean Six Sigma (White or Yellow Belt):** This is a methodology for "process improvement." It is highly valued in QA/CQI roles because it gives you a framework for fixing systemic errors in the 90 agencies you oversee.
    

### 2. Efficiency Tools (The "Soft" Skills)

-   **Project Management Software:** If you aren't using one, a tool like **Trello** or **Asana** can help you track the 90 different licensure statuses and ongoing investigations simultaneously.
    
-   **Data Visualization:** Since you manage CARES and CQI data, learning basic **PowerBI** or advanced **Excel** (Pivot Tables/Charts) will allow you to present data to the MAC in a way that is visually "punchy" and easy for doctors to digest quickly.
    

### 3. Regulatory "Deep Dives"

-   **Legislative Tracking:** Set up alerts for the **Pennsylvania Bulletin**. This is where changes to Title 28 and EMS scope of practice are officially published. Being the first to know about a change makes you an indispensable resource to your 90 agencies.
