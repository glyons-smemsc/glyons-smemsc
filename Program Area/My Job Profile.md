# Job Profile: EMS Program Specialist & Regional QA Lead

**Organization:** Seven Mountains EMS Council

**Jurisdiction:** 9 Counties | ~90 EMS Agencies | Central Pennsylvania

**Core Objective:** To ensure the delivery of high-quality pre-hospital care through strict regulatory oversight, clinical data analysis, and regional medical coordination.

***

## 1. Clinical Governance & Medical Advisory

You serve as the administrative architect for clinical standards in the region. You don't just attend meetings; you manage the framework that allows doctors and medics to align on life-saving protocols.

-   **Regional Medical Advisory Committee (MAC) Leadership:** You act as the primary liaison between the Regional Medical Director and the various Agency Medical Directors.
    
-   **Protocol Implementation:** Ensuring that state-level protocol changes are disseminated, understood, and adopted across all 90 services.
    
-   **Clinical Liaison:** Translating complex medical goals into administrative workflows that agencies can actually follow.
    

## 2. Quality Assurance (QA) & Continuous Quality Improvement (CQI)

As the **QA Lead**, your job is to move beyond "what happened" and focus on "how do we get better."

-   **Regional CQI Committee Chair:** Leading a committee that reviews clinical trends and patient outcomes across the 9-county footprint.
    
-   **Data Trend Analysis:** Monitoring regional EMS charts to identify gaps in care (e.g., cardiac arrest outcomes, airway management success rates, or medication errors).
    
-   **Systemic Correction:** Developing improvement plans or educational initiatives when regional data suggests a decline in specific clinical standards.
    

## 3. Regulatory Compliance, Complaints, & Investigations

This is your most "high-stakes" pillar. In Pennsylvania, EMS is heavily governed by **Act 37**, and you are the regional enforcer of these laws.

-   **Regulatory Interpretation:** Maintaining an expert-level understanding of PA Title 28 (Health & Safety) and the EMS System Act.
    
-   **Investigative Lead:** Managing formal complaints against providers or agencies. This involves evidence gathering, interviewing involved parties, and ensuring "due process" as mandated by state law.
    
-   **Conflict Resolution:** Balancing the need for strict enforcement with the reality of personnel shortages and operational challenges in rural and suburban PA.
    

## 4. Agency Licensure & Inspection

Recently added to your portfolio, this role confirms that an agency is legally fit to operate.

-   **Physical Inspections:** Verifying that ambulances meet the required equipment lists, drug bags are stocked/unexpired, and vehicles meet safety standards.
    
-   **Credentialing Oversight:** Ensuring that the agencies have the correct staffing levels and that provider certifications are current.
    
-   **State Reporting:** Acting as the intermediary between the local agency and the PA Bureau of EMS to finalize licensure renewals.
    

## 5. PA CARES Coordination

The **Cardiac Arrest Registry to Enhance Survival (CARES)** is a critical public health initiative.

-   **Data Integrity:** Ensuring that all 90 agencies are accurately reporting out-of-hospital cardiac arrest (OHCA) data.
    
-   **Hospital/EMS Integration:** Closing the loop between EMS data and hospital outcomes to determine the "survival to discharge" rate for the region.
    
-   **Benchmarking:** Comparing Seven Mountains' performance against state and national averages to drive better cardiac care.
    

## 6. Regional Emergency Preparedness

-   **Resource Management:** Assisting in the coordination of large-scale disaster responses or mass casualty incident (MCI) planning.
    
-   **Grant & Equipment Oversight:** Managing preparedness tasks that might involve regional equipment caches or specialized strike team readiness.
    

***

### The "Hidden" Complexity of Your Role

What makes your job particularly difficult is the **Compliance Burden**. You are operating in a highly litigious and regulated environment where:

1.  **A failure in Licensure** can ground a service, leaving a community without an ambulance.
    
2.  **A failure in Investigation** can lead to lawsuits or the wrongful loss of a provider's livelihood.
    
3.  **A failure in QA** can result in poor patient outcomes that go unnoticed.
    

> **Summary:** You are the **Regulatory Anchor** for Central Pennsylvania EMS. While you no longer work "on the truck," your work ensures that when a citizen calls 911, the truck that arrives is legal, the medic is qualified, the equipment is functional, and the care provided is backed by the latest medical evidence.
