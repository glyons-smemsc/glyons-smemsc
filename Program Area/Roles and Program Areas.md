# **Pennsylvania EMS Councils: Summary of Roles & Responsibilities**

Authorized under **Act 37 of 2009** (the current Pennsylvania EMS Act), EMS Councils are non-profit entities designated by the Pennsylvania Department of Health (PA DOH) to serve as the **primary regional resource for EMS system development, support, and regulation.** They act as the crucial liaison between the state, local government, and the hundreds of EMS agencies and thousands of providers on the ground.

***

## **Core Roles:**

1. **Regional System Management:** Implement state EMS protocols and standards within a defined geographic region (in your case, the Central PA counties served by Seven Mountains).

2. **Technical Assistance & Support:** Provide direct support, guidance, and resources to EMS agencies, hospitals, and municipalities.

3. **Regulatory & Quality Assurance:** Perform key functions on behalf of the PA DOH to ensure compliance and system quality.

4. **Planning & Development:** Identify regional needs and facilitate solutions for system gaps, disasters, and community health needs.

5. **Education & Coordination:** Serve as the regional hub for training coordination and information dissemination.

***

## **Key Responsibilities & Program Activities:**

### 1. Licensing & Inspection

- **Agency Licensing:** Process applications for new EMS agency licenses and renewals.

- **Inspections:** Conduct mandatory annual inspections of licensed EMS agencies for compliance with regulations (vehicle, equipment, drug, records, etc.).

- **Corrective Action:** Investigate complaints and incidents, and monitor agency plans of correction.

### 2. Education & Certification

- **Course Sponsorship & Coordination:** Sponsor and coordinate EMT, AEMT, and Paramedic initial education programs across the region.

- **Continuing Education (CE):** Approve and provide required CE courses to help providers maintain certification.

- **Testing:** Administer state certification practical examinations.

- **Instructor Coordination:** Support and oversee EMS educators within the region.

### 3. Clinical & Quality Improvement

- **Protocol Implementation:** Ensure regional EMS providers are trained on and adhere to the statewide EMS protocols.

- **Quality Assurance/Quality Improvement (QA/QI):** Manage regional medical QI programs, including PCR review, feedback to providers, and trending of clinical data to improve patient care outcomes.

- **Preceptor & Preceptorship Programs:** Coordinate field training for new paramedics.

### 4. Data Management & System Performance

- **Pennsylvania Patient Care Report (PCR) Data:** Support agencies in the use of the statewide electronic PCR (ePCR) system, ensure data submission compliance, and analyze regional data.

- **System Performance Metrics:** Track response times, level of service coverage, and other metrics to identify system stresses and needs.

### 5. Regional EMS System Development (REMSD) & Planning

- **Regional EMS Plans:** Develop, maintain, and implement a strategic plan for the region's EMS system.

- **Resource Management:** Facilitate mutual aid agreements and coordinate resource sharing among agencies.

- **Disaster Preparedness:** Work with county emergency management and DOH on mass casualty incident (MCI) planning and response integration.

- **Coverage & Gap Analysis:** Identify areas of unstable or unavailable EMS service and work with municipalities and agencies to develop solutions.

### 6. Advocacy & Resource Hub

- **Grant Administration:** Often administer state and federal grant funding (e.g., Act 13, EOG) to EMS agencies for equipment, training, and operations.

- **Information Clearinghouse:** Disseminate critical updates from the PA DOH, updates on regulations, and best practices.

- **Municipal Assistance:** Provide technical guidance to municipalities on their legal responsibilities for providing EMS.

### 7. Investigation

- Conduct preliminary investigations of violations of the EMS Act and regulations, reporting findings to the PA DOH Bureau of EMS.
